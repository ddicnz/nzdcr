Why self-drive suits New Zealand

Public transport serves the main centres, but many of the country’s highlights sit between towns—coastal lookouts, short walks, wine regions, and scenic detours. A rental car lets you set your own pace, carry gear comfortably, and change plans if the weather shifts (something that happens often, especially in alpine areas).

Before you arrive

  • Book early in peak season (December–February and major holidays)—demand is high and early booking secures both price and vehicle class.
  • Check your licence: most overseas visitors can drive on a valid English-language licence or an approved translation; allow time to confirm requirements before travel.
  • Plan realistic driving days: New Zealand roads are often narrower and windier than motorways overseas; scenic stops add time in the best way.

Driving tips (quick)

We drive on the left. Take breaks on long stretches, watch for weather on mountain passes, and use rest areas rather than rushing. If you are unsure about winter driving or high-country routes, ask our team when you collect the vehicle—we are happy to point you to current conditions and sensible routes.
