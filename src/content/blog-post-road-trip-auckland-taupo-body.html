Road trip · 30 April 2024

Auckland to Lake Taupo and the volcanic Central Plateau is roughly four hours on State Highway 1 if you push through, but heritage towns, museums, and geothermal sights along the Waikato and king country deserve a slower pace.

Waikato corridor

South from Auckland, Pukekohe blends horticulture with motorsport history at Pukekohe Park. Pokeno is famous for generous ice creams; Meremere and Hampton Downs cater to motoring fans. Te Kauwhata offers greenstone galleries before you follow the Waikato River to Huntly, where a museum outlines coal-era stories opposite the modern power station. Taupiri has family-friendly stops; Hamilton spreads along the river with parks, the Hamilton Gardens, a zoo, and classic car displays.

Cambridge sits among thoroughbred farms; Lake Karāpiro hosts rowing and water sports. Tirau’s corrugated-iron sculptures and quirky shops are a photo favourite. You can fork onto SH5 toward Taupo via Rotorua for geothermal detours, or stay on SH1 through Putaruru (timber museum, cheese tasting, pavement art) and Tokoroa (Talking Poles art trail, lake walkway). The Wairakei steam plumes signal geothermal power and hot pools before Taupo town.

Auckland Airport is our natural base for this journey — free shuttle details are on our airport pages.

Lake Taupo

Taupo combines geothermal walking tracks, lake beaches, cafés, and the massive caldera lake itself. Huka Falls and the Aratiatia dam release are stand-out stops on the Waikato River outflow. Boating, kayaking, fishing, and the Māori rock carving at Mine Bay reward an extra day.

Central Plateau

Following the eastern shore to Tūrangi opens trout fishing and the Tongariro River. SH1 continues over the Desert Road past the army museum at Waiouru, but many travellers branch from Tūrangi on SH47 past Tokaanu hot pools toward Tongariro National Park. The Tongariro Alpine Crossing is a famous one-day trek between volcanoes; Mt Ngāuruhoe and Mt Ruapehu dominate winter skiing from Whakapapa and Tūroa. The Chateau Tongariro building at Whakapapa is a heritage landmark; check Department of Conservation and ski-field notices for volcanic activity and weather closures.
