Road trip · 30 April 2024

Between Christchurch and Queenstown you travel through some of the most photographed landscapes in New Zealand. The drive is a little under 500 km and can be done in around six hours, but if you stop at lookouts and towns it easily becomes a full day — or multi-day — journey.

Canterbury Plains

Leaving Christchurch, you head south across the Canterbury Plains through Rolleston, Burnham (with its military camp), and Dunsandel. Side roads lead to balloon operators over the plains if you want a sunrise flight. At Rakaia the long bridge spans a classic braided river — a favourite photo stop. Ashburton offers museums covering farming, rail, aviation, and motoring history, and the high country toward Mt Hutt leads to Methven, a handy base for Mt Hutt Ski Area. At Orari you leave SH1 toward Geraldine, where a vintage car and machinery museum rewards a pause.

Mackenzie Country

Fairlie marks a shift into the Mackenzie Basin. In winter the area links to Mt Dobson and other nearby fields. On SH8 the land opens onto turquoise glacial lakes and canals where suspended rock flour colours the water. Lake Tekapo has the Church of the Good Shepherd, views toward Aoraki Mt Cook on a clear day, and the sheepdog memorial. Walking, horse trekking, and stargazing experiences are popular in the reserve status night sky around Tekapo.

Aoraki Mt Cook

Near Lake Pukaki the highway meets the turn-off to Aoraki Mount Cook National Park. Mt Cook Village is the hub for glacier and alpine activities, walks, and the museum and visitor centre honouring local climbing history (including Sir Edmund Hillary). When you rejoin the main highway, Twizel sits in open tussock country familiar from many film backdrops; Lake Ruataniwha supports rowing and cycling connections to the Alps 2 Ocean trail. Omarama offers clay cliffs and high-country cafés before the Lindis Pass crossing, a stretch many drivers rate among the prettiest on the route. A detour on SH8A leads to Wānaka and its lake; time it right for air-show weekends.

Goldfields and Kawarau Gorge

Back on SH8 you follow Lake Dunstan to Cromwell: much of the old town now lies under the lake, while heritage buildings were preserved at Old Cromwell. Stone-fruit stalls and cellar doors make good seasonal stops. The road then threads the Kawarau Gorge past the Goldfields Mining Centre and Roaring Meg power scheme toward the historic Kawarau suspension bridge — home of commercial bungy jumping. Beyond the gorge, Queenstown appears above Lake Wakatipu with the Remarkables range as a backdrop.
