Road trip · 24 May 2024

Many visitors leave Auckland straight away for the South Island and skip the far north — yet Northland holds early colonial stories, subtropical scenery, and two classic road corridors that combine into a superb loop around the upper North Island.

Route 1 — west and the Kauri Coast

Head west from Auckland on SH16 through Kumeū wine country and Helensville; thermal pools at Parakai make an easy break. North through Kaukapakapa and Wellsford you pass the Kaipara Coast Sculpture Gardens. From Wellsford, branch left onto SH12 via Matakohe (Kauri Museum), Dargaville, and the Kai Iwi freshwater dune lakes. Waipoua Forest holds giants such as Tāne Mahuta; night tours may offer kiwi spotting near smaller settlements. The Hokianga ferry at Kohukohu shortcuts to the forest road north toward Kaitāia; Awanui is the gateway to Cape Reinga tours. Gumdiggers Park and The Kauri Kingdom are popular stops before the final stretch north.

Important: Ninety Mile Beach is a public highway only in places and has soft sand and tidal risks. Most rental agreements do not allow driving hired vehicles on the beach. Many visitors take an organised coach tour from Kaitāia instead of risking vehicle damage or a breach of contract.

At Cape Reinga (Te Rerenga Wairua) you can watch the Tasman Sea and Pacific meet below the lighthouse. On the return leg, Opononi and the Hokianga, and the Wairere Boulders near Horeke, are worthwhile detours.

Route 2 — east coast and the Bay of Islands

Alternatively, take SH1 through Silverdale and Orewa; thermal pools at Waiwera and the café scene at Warkworth lead to Goat Island Marine Reserve and Sheepworld. Wellsford is a handy meal stop before Kaiwaka and the turnoff to Mangawhai and Waipū (Scottish heritage, caves, surf). Ruakāka fronts Whangārei harbour; Whangārei itself offers waterfalls, kauri walks, and the harbour heads. Coastal roads toward Russell and the Bay of Islands pass Tutukaka, the historic treaty settlement at Waitangi, and Kerikeri’s Stone Store and mission buildings. Paihia is the main jumping-off point for cruises, the Hole in the Rock, and dolphin trips. Farther north, Mangonui and Doubtless Bay lead back toward the shared northern section with Route 1 at Awanui.
