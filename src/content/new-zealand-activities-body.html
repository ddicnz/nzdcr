You’re spoilt for choice with ocean, lake and river cruises and charters available throughout New Zealand for all types of boats, budgets and sailors. Including top-end skippered luxury cruises to fishing charters in some of the world’s best fishing spots, plus sailing schools to get you started….

NZ Fishing

With some of the best fishing in the world, New Zealand is a great place for anglers of all skill and experience levels. Choose from freshwater fishing, inshore saltwater fishing and big game fishing. Fly fishing, salmon and trout fishing are hugely popular with visitors from all over the world. Fishing guides and charters are available…

If you are a keen botanist, keen amateur gardener or just someone who enjoys a beautiful place, then why not visit some of the public parks and private gardens open to visitors throughout New Zealand. NZ Glaciers

New Zealand’s most famous glaciers are the Fox and Franz Josef glaciers on the West Coast of the South Island. You can appreciate the beauty and power of these glaciers from land and air and find out more about them from local guides. Popular activities on or nearby the glaciers include walking, ice-hiking and climbing…

NZ Golf, Golf Courses and Golf Cross

There are hundreds of golf courses in New Zealand varying in sizes and catering for golf masters to give-it-a-go beginner golfers…

Experience and learn more about New Zealand’s settlement and colonial past, heritage and history by visiting the many heritage parks, museums, historic buildings, restored pioneer settlements and historical villages, factories and breweries, gold mining centres, missionary houses and New Zealand’s only castle (Larnach Castle, Dunedin)…

With over 100 natural hot springs in New Zealand, you are never far away from a relaxing soak in hot, mineral waters. NZ Māori Culture

In the Māori language, New Zealand is called Aotearoa meaning Land of the Long White Cloud. In this land, Māori culture, language and tradition is part of every day life and you can experience it (and participate in it) through concerts, hangi, arts and crafts and Marae visits (meeting places)…
