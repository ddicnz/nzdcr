Road trip · 30 April 2024

Linking the North and South Island capitals pairs a scenic Cook Strait ferry with about 340 km of coastal and inland driving — typically eight or nine hours all up, including a crossing of a little over three hours on the main Interislander service (Bluebridge is an alternative). Check-in times and vehicle height rules apply; book sailing times in advance in peak season.

Rental cars: if your hire agreement does not clearly allow crossing Cook Strait with the vehicle, confirm with your rental company before boarding. Policies differ on ferries, surcharges, and paperwork.

Crossing Cook Strait

Leaving Wellington, ferries thread Tory Channel and Queen Charlotte Sound, often rated among the world’s prettiest sailings. Keep cameras ready for the Marlborough Sounds forested shorelines.

Picton and Marlborough Sounds

Picton showcases maritime history: the Edwin Fox ship museum recalls immigrant and cargo voyages under sail. The Marlborough Sounds invite cruises, kayaking, and mountain-bike trails. A side trip to Nelson crosses windy saddles toward orchards, craft studios, and beaches.

Blenheim and wine country

Blenheim anchors New Zealand’s largest wine region, especially sauvignon blanc. Omaka Aviation Heritage Centre near the airfield displays strikingly staged historic aircraft.

Kaikōura coast

Fur seals often loaf near the highway south of Clarence. Kaikōura sits between mountains and deep ocean trenches, famous for whale-watch cruises and flights, dolphin encounters, crayfish meals, and short walks to headland viewpoints. Fyffe House, perched on old whalebone piles, recalls the town’s shore-whaling past.

After Kaikōura, SH70 inland via Waiau reaches Hanmer Springs thermal pools; the main highway SH1 continues through Cheviot and crosses braided rivers with fishing and jet-boat opportunities. Waipara Valley offers wineries, art studios, and the Weka Pass heritage railway; Amberley blends agriculture with a nearby beach.

Kaiāpoi and Christchurch

Kaiāpoi on the Waimakariri River keeps Willowbank Wildlife Reserve, where you can see kiwi, kea, kākā, takahē, and tuatara among other species. Suburbs then lead into Christchurch, with English riverfront heritage and the Southern Alps on the western skyline.
